## 1. 🔍 Overview of Differences

The main difference for the Western Dolomites is **efficiency and utilizing your final days**. 
- The **Google Sheet** has a very light transit day (just a 2hr drive with Lagazuoi) and a very weak final day on Sept 24 ("super slow day"). It also proposes a serious, technical Via Ferrata (Tunnel) on Marmolada without factoring in the huge time and gear commitment.
- The **ET-Optimized Plan** turns your transit day into an epic scenic drive (adding the Marmolada cable car and Sellaronda passes) and replaces your empty final day with one of the most stunning hikes in the region (Puez-Odle Altopiano).

## 2. ⚖️ What's Missing in Each Plan?

### 🟢 In the ET-Optimized Plan (Missing from Google Sheet)
* **Puez-Odle Altopiano Hike**: The absolute best way to spend your empty Sept 24. A spectacular 17km downhill/rolling hike starting from the Dantercepies gondola.
* **Santa Maddalena Church Viewpoint**: A tiny 5-minute detour added to your Val di Funes day for the iconic postcard photo of the Dolomites.
* **The "Sellaronda" Drive**: ET explicitly routes your transit day to hit Passo Fedaia, Passo Pordoi, and Sella Pass.

### 🟡 In the Google Sheet (Missing from ET Plan)
* **Paragliding**: You listed this on Sept 20. Val Gardena is a great place for it, but you'll need to book early and do it in the morning before thermals get messy.
* **Via Ferrata (Tunnel / Piccolo)**: You listed these technical climbing routes. The ET plan focuses purely on hiking/driving. *(Note: If you do a Via Ferrata, you need a harness, helmet, and ferrata clip set).*

## 3. 🤝 Common Activities
Both itineraries agree on the Western heavyweights:
* **Seceda Ridge** (the famous jagged peaks plunging into the valley).
* **Seiser Alm / Alpe di Siusi** (the largest high-altitude alpine meadow in Europe).
* **Val di Funes & Adolf Munkel Weg** (hiking beneath the Geisler/Odle peaks).
* **Castelrotto** (afternoon medieval village visit).
