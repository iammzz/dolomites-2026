# 🏔️ Eastern Dolomites Itinerary Comparison

This document explicitly compares the **Eastern Dolomites (Cortina d'Ampezzo base)** portion of your two planning sources:

---

## 1. 🔍 Overview of Differences

The most significant difference between the two itineraries is **pacing**. 
- The **Google Sheet** crams a massive amount of activity into single days (e.g., Sept 14 has Cinque Torri, Passo Giau, Rifugio Averau, Lagazuoi, and the Freccia nel Cielo cable car all in one day), while leaving Sept 17 completely blank.
- The **Current Plan** spreads the Eastern Dolomites over dedicated, manageable days (e.g., dedicating a whole day to Cinque Torri/Giau, and a whole day to Lago di Sorapis), making it far more realistic for mountain travel.

## 2. ⚖️ What's Missing in Each Plan?

* **Lago di Federa (Croda da Lago Loop)**: The Google Sheet leaves Thursday (Sept 17) completely empty. Your current plan smartly fills one of your days with the Lago di Federa hike. It is a stunning, less-touristy alternative to the major lakes.

### 🟡 In Google Sheet (Missing from Current Plan)

* **Lagazuoi Open Air Museum / Kaiserjäger Path**: Mentioned in the Sheet on the transit day (Sept 19). An incredible WWI tunnel complex.

## 4. 🥾 Master Activity List (Eastern Dolomites)

Combining both sources, here is the ultimate master list of activities for your Cortina basecamp, evaluated and categorized.

| Activity | Hike Distance | Intensity | View Payoff | Must-Do Level | Description / Notes |
|----------|---------------|-----------|-------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Tre Cime di Lavaredo Loop** | 10 km loop | Moderate | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🔴 Absolute Must | The iconic three peaks. Relatively flat but long. Starts at Rifugio Auronzo (requires toll road/early arrival). |
| **Cadini di Misurina Viewpoint** | 3 km return | Moderate | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🟠 Highly Recommend | Dramatic jagged peaks and a famous photo spot. Starts from the exact same parking lot as Tre Cime. Short but exposed ledges. |
| **Cinque Torri & Rifugio Nuvolau** | 4-5 km loop | Easy / Mod | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🔴 Absolute Must | Explore WWI trenches among massive stone towers. Take the chairlift up from Baita Bai de Dones, then hike up to Nuvolau for 360° views. |
| **Lago di Sorapis** | 12 km return | Hard | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🟠 Highly Recommend | Famous for its opaque, milky-blue glacial water. The hike has steep inclines, metal staircases, and exposed cliff-side cables. Not for those with vertigo. |
| **Pragser Wildsee (Lago di Braies)** | 3.7 km loop | Easy | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🟠 Highly Recommend | Emerald green lake surrounded by peaks. Extremely crowded. Flat walking loop or rent a rowboat. *Must book parking/shuttles months in advance.* |
| **Lagazuoi (WWI Tunnels)** | 4-6 km (varies) | Mod / Hard | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🟠 Highly Recommend | Take the cable car up from Falzarego Pass. You can explore the dizzying descent through pitch-black WWI tunnels carved inside the mountain (bring headlamps). |
| **Lago di Federa (Croda da Lago)** | 12.5 km loop | Hard | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🟡 Great Addition | A beautiful lake reflecting the Croda da Lago peaks. Surrounded by larch trees. A great, quieter alternative to Sorapis. |
| **Passo Giau** | N/A (Drive) | Easy | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 🟠 Highly Recommend | 2,236m mountain pass. Incredible scenic drive. No hiking required, just park and take photos (great for sunset). |
| **Freccia nel Cielo (Cima Tofana)** | N/A (Cable car) | Easy | ⭐⭐⭐ | 🟡 Optional | Three-stage cable car to the highest peak overlooking Cortina. Great if you want high altitude without hiking, but pricey. |
| **Lago di Dobbiaco** | 2.5 km loop | Easy | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⚪ If passing by | A nice, quieter alpine lake north of Cortina. Great for a relaxed lunch stop after Lago di Braies. |
