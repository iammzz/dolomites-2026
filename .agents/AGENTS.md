# Agent Behavioral Rules

*   When a user marks tasks as done or provides evidence of completed tasks (like receipts), always cross-check against the project's to-do list (`docs/todo.md` and/or `TODO.md`) and automatically update it to reflect the completion status.
*   When new logistics or bookings (like car rentals, flights, or activities) are provided, do not just update the logistics summary. Always identify the relevant daily itinerary files (e.g., `docs/dayX_*.md`) and recalibrate the timeline, start times, and events for those days to match the exact booking details.
*   Whenever a change is made to the itinerary (e.g., changing daily plans, a new logistics booking, adding a new leg or shifting days), ensure that the overarching documentation files (`docs/index.md`, `docs/todo.md`, `docs/logistics.md`, `docs/potential_activities.md`, `questions.md`, and the daily activities files) are all kept in sync.
*   **Global Banner Sync**: You MUST actively scan for and update any "CRITICAL ACTION REQUIRED" or global status banners at the top of `docs/todo.md` and `docs/index.md` whenever a booking is completed. Never leave a banner claiming something is unbooked when the checkbox list shows it is booked.
*   CRITICAL: The Welcome page (`docs/index.md`) serves as the master overview of the entire trip. If ANY changes are made to daily itinerary files (e.g., shifting a car rental pickup from Day 1 to Day 2), you MUST explicitly verify and update the corresponding entry in `docs/index.md` so it never falls out of sync with the detailed daily pages.
*   **Auto-Push Policy**: Whenever you make any modifications to the website content, documentation, or configuration (e.g. `docs/`, `mkdocs.yml`, etc.), you must automatically commit those changes and run `git push` to keep the live repository updated without requiring a separate prompt from the user.
*   **Dining Enforcement**: Whenever a new day is created or an existing day's location/activities change significantly, the agent MUST automatically generate or update a "🍽️ Dining & Restaurant Options" section at the bottom of the page. This section must include 5-6 curated options featuring: Drive distance, Food type, Typical price, Google Maps link, and a summary of why it's recommended (prioritizing unique/hyped local food).
*   **Activity Detail & Sourcing Rule**: When outlining activities in daily itineraries, agents must NOT use generic placeholders or basic bullet points. Agents must conduct deep web research for every activity to provide: (1) Granular time estimates that separate physical exertion (hiking/walking) from stationary lounging/enjoyment, (2) Highly actionable logistics including specific parking fees, trailhead names, and access restrictions, and (3) Exact, direct URLs to legitimate, specific guide resources (e.g., direct blog post URLs, direct AllTrails paths) rather than proxy search redirects.
*   **Gear Scanning & Sync**: Whenever a change is made to the daily itineraries or new activities are added, the agent MUST actively scan for any special gear or equipment requirements mentioned (such as via ferrata gear, trekking poles, headlamps, cold weather layers, or cash requirements). If new gear requirements are identified, the agent must automatically update and synchronize the master packing list (`docs/packing_list.md`) accordingly to keep it updated.
