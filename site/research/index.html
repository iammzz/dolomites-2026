    🔍 Research & Insights

    This page acts as our central research repository for the Dolomites 2026 trip. It is used to analyze costs, permits, routing, and logistics options to ensure we make data-driven decisions.

    🚡 Gardena Card Cost-Benefit Analysis (Summer 2026)

    Resource Intake: Val Gardena Summer Lifts Poster 2026 (PDF)

    📋 Key Facts & Prices

    Based on the official 2026 Val Gardena summer pricing:

    • Gardena Card Options (Consecutive Days):
      • 3 Consecutive Days: €124.00 per adult
      • 6 Consecutive Days: €160.00 per adult
      • Note: The card includes unlimited lift access on covered lifts, but does not include bike transport (bike tickets cost €3.00 to €7.00 extra per lift).
    • Individual Lift Ticket Prices (Summer 2026):
      • Ortisei - Furnes - Seceda (Round-trip): €74.00
      • Ortisei - Alpe di Siusi (Round-trip): €41.00
      • Dantercepies (Round-trip): €28.00 (One-way: €19.00)
      • Col Raiser (Round-trip): €34.00
      • Resciesa (Round-trip): €34.00
    • Important Lift Caveat:
      • Many key lifts (including Seceda, Dantercepies, Col Raiser, and Resciesa) carry the NO DSS tag on the poster, meaning the Dolomiti SuperSummer Card is NOT valid on these lifts. Only the local Gardena Card or individual tickets can be used.

    📊 Scenario Analysis

    Our group consists of 5 Adults staying in Val Gardena from Sept 13 to Sept 18. The 3-day window for our planned lift activities would be Sept 15, 16, and 17 (Days 4, 5, and 6).

    Option 1: Stick to Planned Itinerary (Driving to Alpe di Siusi)

    • Day 4 (Alpe di Siusi): The group drives up to the Compatsch P2 parking lot before the 9:00 AM road closure.
      • Cost: €30.00 parking per car (approx. €6.00 per person). No lift tickets needed.
    • Day 5 (Seceda): Everyone rides the Seceda cable car.
      • Cost: €74.00 per person.
    • Day 6 (West Split Day):
      • Group A (Thrill-Seekers - 3 people): Scramble up Gran Cir via Dantercepies gondola return.
        • Cost: €28.00 per person.
      • Group B (Relaxers - 2 people): Explore town center/spa. No lifts used.
        • Cost: €0.00.
    Option 1 Totals:
    • Thrill-Seekers (Group A): €6.00 (parking share) + €74.00 (Seceda) + €28.00 (Dantercepies) = €108.00
    • Relaxers (Group B): €6.00 (parking share) + €74.00 (Seceda) = €80.00
    • Group Total Cost: (3 * €108.00) + (2 * €80.00) = €484.00

    Option 2: Avoid Early Drive & Take Alpe di Siusi Gondola from Ortisei

    If we choose not to drive to Compatsch before 9:00 AM on Day 4, we must take the Ortisei-Alpe di Siusi gondola up.

    • Day 4 (Alpe di Siusi Gondola): €41.00 per person.
    • Day 5 (Seceda Cable Car): €74.00 per person.
    • Day 6 (Dantercepies): €28.00 per person (Group A only).
    Option 2 Totals (Using Individual Tickets):
    • Thrill-Seekers (Group A): €41.00 + €74.00 + €28.00 = €143.00 (buying the €124 Gardena Card saves €19.00 per person).
    • Relaxers (Group B): €41.00 + €74.00 = €115.00 (buying the €124 Gardena Card would lose €9.00 per person).
    • Group Total Cost (Best Mix of Cards/Tickets): (3 * €124.00 Gardena Card) + (2 * €115.00 individual tickets) = €602.00

    💡 Conclusion & Recommendation

    [!IMPORTANT] We should NOT buy the Gardena Card. Sticking to our original plan (Option 1) of driving up to Compatsch before 9:00 AM and buying individual tickets is the most economical and flexible path.

    1. Direct Cost Savings: Option 1 saves our group €118.00 compared to Option 2 with the Gardena Card (Group Total of €484.00 vs. €602.00).
    2. Weather Flexibility: The Gardena Card requires use on consecutive days. Weather in the Dolomites is highly unpredictable. If it rains or is foggy on Day 5, we can easily postpone our Seceda trip to Day 6 without wasting money. If we had the Gardena Card, we would be locked into our days regardless of visibility.
    3. Car Convenience: Having the rental car at Compatsch on Day 4 allows us to stay on the plateau for the sunset and drive down at our own pace without worrying about catching the last gondola down.
