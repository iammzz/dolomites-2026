    1. Croda da Lago Circuit (Lago Federa)

    Earth Trekkers explicitly calls Croda da Lago "one of the quietest trails in the Dolomites" — a perfect, intentional contrast after the massive crowds of Tre Cime and Sorapis. You hike through a forest to a stunning glacial lake with the sheer cliffs of Becco di Mezzodì towering above it.

    • Distance: 12.5–13.5 km loop
    • Elevation Change: ~870–950m
    • Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Moderately Strenuous
    • Duration: 4.5–6 hrs
    • Extensions: You can make this hike shorter by doing an out-and-back just to Lago Federa, skipping the Forcella Ambrizzola and Val Formin descent.
    • Resources:
    • Earth Trekkers: Croda da Lago Circuit
    • AllTrails: Croda da Lago
    • Notes: Hike clockwise: Forest ascent → Lago Federa (2,046 m) → Rifugio Croda da Lago → Forcella Ambrizzola (2,277 m) → Val Formin descent. The Val Formin descent is rocky and technical—trekking poles are essential.

    Logistics

    • Trailhead: Ponte di Rocurto
    • Drive Time from Cortina: ~15-20 min (10-12 km)
    • Parking — CRITICAL: Ponte di Rocurto has only 3–4 parking spaces. Arrive by 7:30 am at the latest. Bus alternative: Line 30/4 from Cortina or Pescul.
    • Rifugio Croda da Lago: Typically open June–October. Confirm opening status before relying on it for lunch.

    2. Lago di Braies (Pragser Wildsee)

    Lago di Braies is an emerald jewel surrounded by towering peaks. Because of overtourism, access is strictly regulated. You must visit at dawn to get the magical morning light, beat the crowds, and actually be allowed to drive there.

    • Distance: 3.6–4.5 km loop (flat) + 30 min rowboat
    • Elevation Change: ~40m
    • Difficulty: ⭐ Easy
    • Duration: 1–2 hrs walk + 30–60 min rowboat
    • Extensions: Hike up to Grünwaldalm (Malga Foresta) from the far side of the lake for an extra ~3km round trip.
    • Resources:
    • Earth Trekkers: Lago di Braies
    • AllTrails: Lago di Braies Loop
    • Notes: Do the rowboat first at 8:30am before the queue builds. Then walk the full perimeter trail. Best photos are from the east bank looking back at the water.

    Logistics

    • ROAD ACCESS CRITICAL: The road to Lago di Braies is closed to private vehicles 9:30am–4:00pm during peak season. You MUST arrive before 9:30am with pre-booked parking (P4) at prags.bz. Do not leave Cortina later than 7:30am sharp.
    • Drive Time from Cortina: ~55-60 min (47 km)
    • Rowboat Queue: Go directly to the dock at 8:30am on arrival. Queues build quickly.

    3. Lago di Dobbiaco & Lago di Misurina

    These lakes were originally scheduled on the same loop as Lago di Braies.

    • Lago di Dobbiaco: Quieter than Braies and popular with locals. The south end has the best mountain reflections.
    • Vista Panoramica Tre Cime: GPS approx 46°38'N 12°16'E on SS51. A completely different telephoto view of the Tre Cime north face.
    • Lago di Misurina: A relaxed wind-down stop. 2–3 km partial loop (flat). Altitude 1,756m.

    4. Piz Boé Summit

    Day 9 — Monday, Sept 21 🏔️

    High Altitude Challenge: Piz Boé Summit

    Base: Val Gardena (Ortisei/Selva)

    ✅ Main Activity: Bagging a 3,000m Peak

    Expectation & Rationale: Piz Boé (3,152m) is widely considered one of the easiest 3,000-meter peaks to summit in the Dolomites, thanks to the Sass Pordoi cable car which lifts you to 2,950m before you even start hiking. The landscape up here is stark, lunar, and completely barren rock. It's a challenging, rocky climb that requires some light scrambling, but the reward is standing on the highest peak of the Sella Group.

    1. Piz Boé Summit Hike

    • Distance: 5 km round trip
    • Elevation Change: ~400m
    • Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Strenuous (High altitude, rocky scrambling, some fixed cables)
    • Duration: 3–4 hours
    • Extensions: You can hike back down via a different trail to Rifugio Boé to make it a loop before returning to the cable car.
    • Resources:
    • Earth Trekkers: Piz Boe
    • AllTrails: Sass Pordoi to Piz Boe
    • Notes: Altitude is a real factor here. You are starting the hike at nearly 10,000 feet. Take it slow. There are short sections of fixed cables where you must pull yourself up, but via ferrata gear is not strictly required.

    • Flexibility: If the weather is bad or your legs are shot, skip the summit. You can just ride the cable car up, enjoy the view from the terrace, and then explore Val Gardena by car.

    5. Puez-Odle Altopiano

    Day 10 — Tuesday, Sept 22 🏔️

    The Epic Traverse: Puez-Odle Altopiano

    Base: Val Gardena (Ortisei/Selva)

    ✅ Main Activity: The Best Day Hike in Val Gardena

    Expectation & Rationale: Earth Trekkers considers this the single best hike in the Val Gardena region. It is a long, point-to-point journey across a massive, rocky alpine plateau with sweeping views in every direction. By taking the Dantercepies gondola up from Selva, you eliminate the massive initial climb, meaning most of your 17km day will be spent cruising along scenic ridges and gradually descending back into the valley.

    1. Puez-Odle Altopiano Hike

    • Distance: 17 km (Point-to-point)
    • Elevation Change: +400m ascent, -1,050m descent
    • Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ Moderate to Strenuous (Due to distance)
    • Duration: 6–8 hours
    • Extensions: Detour to the Col de Puez summit (+1 hour).
    • Resources:
    • Earth Trekkers: Puez-Odle Altopiano
    • AllTrails: Puez-Odle Traverse
    • Notes: This is a point-to-point hike. You start at the top of the Dantercepies gondola in Selva, hike across the plateau to Rifugio Puez (perfect lunch stop), and hike all the way down Vallunga valley, ending right back in Selva.

    • Trail Conditions: The first half is high, exposed, rocky plateau. The second half is a long descent through a lush valley. Protect your knees on the descent!

    6. Flex Day Options

    Day 11 — Wednesday, Sept 23 🏔️

    Flex Day 1

    Base: Val Gardena (Ortisei/Selva)

    ✅ Main Activity: Choose Your Own Adventure

    Expectation & Rationale: Because we shifted your itinerary and ended the Cortina leg early, you have gained two extra "Flex Days" in Val Gardena. After completing a 17km hike yesterday, today is a perfect day to let your legs dictate the schedule.

    Option 1: E-Bike the Sella Ronda

    Rent e-mountain bikes in Selva and ride the famous Sella Ronda circuit. The e-bikes do the heavy lifting, allowing you to cover massive ground, cross four mountain passes, and enjoy continuous downhill runs.

    Option 2: The Sassolungo Loop (Hike)

    If you are still eager for hiking, take the cable car up to Ciampinoi and hike the perimeter of the massive Sassolungo group. It's a relatively flat loop that wraps around the base of the towering peaks. * Resources: Earth Trekkers: Sassolungo Loop

    Option 3: Via Ferrata Gran Cir (Introductory Climbing)

    If you want to try a Via Ferrata without fully committing to a massive technical route, Gran Cir is a fantastic beginner route accessible from Passo Gardena. Rent a harness, via ferrata set, and helmet in town. * Resources: Earth Trekkers: Gran Cir

    Option 4: Pure Relaxation

    Spend the day in Ortisei. Visit the Mar Dolomit public pools, book a spa treatment at a local hotel, and shop along the pedestrian center.

    7. Valley Relaxation

    Flex Day 2 / Final Evening

    Base: Val Gardena (Ortisei/Selva)

    ✅ Main Activity: Winding Down in Val Gardena

    Expectation & Rationale: This is your final full day in the Dolomites. Tomorrow is a pure travel day driving all the way back to Venice and flying to Madrid. Keep today simple, relaxed, and localized to the valley to make packing easier.

    • Easy Valley Walk: Walk the paved Val Gardena Railway Trail (a former train track turned pedestrian path) connecting Ortisei, Santa Cristina, and Selva.
    • Packing & Logistics: Clean the rental car, pack your bags, and check out early if needed for tomorrow's 4-hour drive to VCE.

    8. Torri del Vajolet (Vajolet Towers) Hike

    The Vajolet Towers are three sheer spires rising dramatically out of the Rosengarten (Catinaccio) massif. The hike to the base is a classic Dolomite experience, offering a steep and rocky challenge with a monumental visual payoff.

    • Distance: 8 km round trip (using lifts)
    • Elevation Change: ~650m
    • Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Strenuous (Includes a steep, rocky scramble up a gorge using some steel cables for balance near the top).
    • Duration: 4.5 hrs total (Approx. 1.5 hrs hiking from the lift to Rifugio Vajolet, 1 hr steep scramble to the towers at Rifugio Re Alberto, 45 min enjoying the towers, and 1.5 hrs returning).
    • The Route: Take the lifts from Pera di Fassa (Vajolet 1 and 2 chairlifts) or the Vigo-Ciampedie cable car. Walk to Rifugio Gardeccia, then follow trail 546 up to Rifugio Vajolet. From there, take the steep, rocky scramble up trail 542 to Rifugio Re Alberto right at the base of the towers.
    • Resources:
    • The Common Wanderer: Vajolet Towers Guide
    • Anywhere We Roam: Vajolet Towers
    • Notes: Very steep; hiking boots with solid ankle support and good grip are mandatory. Not recommended in wet or icy weather as the rocky gorge can be slippery.

    9. Viel del Pan (Bindelweg)

    A historic trade path running along a high grassy ridge directly opposite the Marmolada Glacier. Because the trail stays high, it delivers continuous, jaw-dropping views of the glacier and Lake Fedaia below with minimal climbing.

    • Distance: 7.5 km point-to-point (Passo Pordoi to Lago di Fedaia)
    • Elevation Change: ~350m ascent, ~700m descent
    • Difficulty: ⭐⭐ Moderate
    • Duration: 3.5 hrs total (Approx. 2.5 hrs hiking, plus 1 hr stopping at Rifugio Viel dal Pan for lunch or coffee).
    • Logistics: Park at Passo Pordoi (near the Sass Pordoi cable car). Hike trail 601 past Rifugio Baita Fredarola to Rifugio Viel dal Pan. Descend down to Lake Fedaia. To get back to Passo Pordoi, take the local SAD bus Line 472 (or Line 130) from the lake.
    • Resources:
    • Moon & Honey Travel: Viel del Pan Guide
    • Notes: A popular trail for mountain bikers; stay alert. Easy footing compared to other high-altitude routes, making it a great option if your legs are tired but you still want big views.

    10. Lago di Carezza (Karersee)

    Known as the "Rainbow Lake," this is a small, highly protected alpine lake that perfectly mirrors the jagged Latemar peaks. The forest surrounding the lake is incredibly photogenic.

    • Distance: 1.2 km flat loop
    • Elevation Change: Negligible
    • Difficulty: ⭐ Easy
    • Duration: 45 mins total (Approx. 25 min walking the perimeter trail, plus 20 min taking photos from the wooden platforms).
    • Logistics: Located in Eggental (Val d'Ega), about 40 min drive from Bolzano. Paid parking at P1 (Karersee) directly at the lake (€5 for 1 hr). Arrive before 9:00 AM or after 5:00 PM to avoid heavy tour bus crowds.
    • Resources:
    • Eggental Tourism: Lago di Carezza Guide
    • Notes: Swimming, boating, and crossing the wooden barriers are strictly prohibited and subject to heavy fines. Drones are also banned to protect the local wildlife.

    11. Val Fiscalina (Fischleintal)

    A quiet, stunning valley in the Sesto (Sexten) Dolomites. It serves as a gentle, picturesque alternative to the high-elevation climbs, running through larch forests and green pastures directly below the towering Sundial Peaks.

    • Distance: 4.5 km round trip (to Rifugio Fondovalle)
    • Elevation Change: ~100m
    • Difficulty: ⭐ Easy (flat, gravel path suitable for all fitness levels)
    • Duration: 1.5 hrs total (Approx. 1 hr walking, plus 30 min at Rifugio Fondovalle for coffee and cake).
    • Logistics: Located near Sesto/Moso. Parking is available at the valley entrance near Hotel Dolomitenhof. Note that from mid-June to early October, the road is closed to private vehicles from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM (access is via the local shuttle bus).
    • Resources:
    • Moon & Honey Travel: Val Fiscalina Guide
    • Notes: A fantastic, peaceful recovery-day option. For those wanting a challenge, you can continue past the hut up the steep trail to Rifugio Locatelli (Three Peaks).
