## 1. Croda da Lago Circuit (Lago Federa)

Earth Trekkers explicitly calls Croda da Lago "one of the quietest trails in the Dolomites" — a perfect, intentional contrast after the massive crowds of Tre Cime and Sorapis. You hike through a forest to a stunning glacial lake with the sheer cliffs of Becco di Mezzodì towering above it. 

* **Distance**: 12.5–13.5 km loop
* **Elevation Change**: ~870–950m
* **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Moderately Strenuous
* **Duration**: 4.5–6 hrs
* **Extensions**: You can make this hike shorter by doing an out-and-back just to Lago Federa, skipping the Forcella Ambrizzola and Val Formin descent.
* **Resources**:
  * [Earth Trekkers: Croda da Lago Circuit](https://www.earthtrekkers.com/best-hikes-in-the-dolomites/)
  * [AllTrails: Croda da Lago](https://www.alltrails.com/explore?b_q=croda%20da%20lago%20lago%20di%20federa)
* **Notes**: Hike clockwise: Forest ascent → Lago Federa (2,046 m) → Rifugio Croda da Lago → Forcella Ambrizzola (2,277 m) → Val Formin descent. The Val Formin descent is rocky and technical—trekking poles are essential.

### Logistics
* **Trailhead**: Ponte di Rocurto
* **Drive Time from Cortina**: ~15-20 min (10-12 km)
* **Parking — CRITICAL**: Ponte di Rocurto has **only 3–4 parking spaces**. Arrive by **7:30 am at the latest**. Bus alternative: Line 30/4 from Cortina or Pescul.
* **Rifugio Croda da Lago**: Typically open June–October. Confirm opening status before relying on it for lunch.

## 2. Lago di Braies (Pragser Wildsee)

Lago di Braies is an emerald jewel surrounded by towering peaks. Because of overtourism, **access is strictly regulated**. You must visit at dawn to get the magical morning light, beat the crowds, and actually be allowed to drive there.

* **Distance**: 3.6–4.5 km loop (flat) + 30 min rowboat
* **Elevation Change**: ~40m
* **Difficulty**: ⭐ Easy
* **Duration**: 1–2 hrs walk + 30–60 min rowboat
* **Extensions**: Hike up to Grünwaldalm (Malga Foresta) from the far side of the lake for an extra ~3km round trip.
* **Resources**:
  * [Earth Trekkers: Lago di Braies](https://www.earthtrekkers.com/best-hikes-in-the-dolomites/)
  * [AllTrails: Lago di Braies Loop](https://www.alltrails.com/explore?b_q=giro%20del%20lago%20di%20braies)
* **Notes**: Do the rowboat first at 8:30am before the queue builds. Then walk the full perimeter trail. Best photos are from the east bank looking back at the water.

### Logistics
* **ROAD ACCESS CRITICAL:** The road to Lago di Braies is **closed to private vehicles 9:30am–4:00pm** during peak season. You MUST arrive before 9:30am with **pre-booked parking (P4)** at [prags.bz](https://www.prags.bz). Do not leave Cortina later than 7:30am sharp.
* **Drive Time from Cortina**: ~55-60 min (47 km)
* **Rowboat Queue**: Go directly to the dock at 8:30am on arrival. Queues build quickly.

## 4. Piz Boé Summit

# Day 9 — Monday, Sept 21 🏔️ 
## High Altitude Challenge: Piz Boé Summit
**Base:** Val Gardena (Ortisei/Selva)


---

## ✅ Main Activity: Bagging a 3,000m Peak

**Expectation & Rationale:** 
Piz Boé (3,152m) is widely considered one of the easiest 3,000-meter peaks to summit in the Dolomites, thanks to the Sass Pordoi cable car which lifts you to 2,950m before you even start hiking. The landscape up here is stark, lunar, and completely barren rock. It's a challenging, rocky climb that requires some light scrambling, but the reward is standing on the highest peak of the Sella Group. 

### 1. Piz Boé Summit Hike
* **Distance**: 5 km round trip
* **Elevation Change**: ~400m
* **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Strenuous (High altitude, rocky scrambling, some fixed cables)
* **Duration**: 3–4 hours
* **Extensions**: You can hike back down via a different trail to Rifugio Boé to make it a loop before returning to the cable car.
* **Resources**: 
  * [Earth Trekkers: Piz Boe](https://www.earthtrekkers.com/best-hikes-in-the-dolomites/)
  * [AllTrails: Sass Pordoi to Piz Boe](https://www.alltrails.com/explore?b_q=sass%20pordoi%20piz%20boe)
* **Notes**: Altitude is a real factor here. You are starting the hike at nearly 10,000 feet. Take it slow. There are short sections of fixed cables where you must pull yourself up, but via ferrata gear is not strictly required.

* **Flexibility**: If the weather is bad or your legs are shot, skip the summit. You can just ride the cable car up, enjoy the view from the terrace, and then explore Val Gardena by car.

## 5. Puez-Odle Altopiano

# Day 10 — Tuesday, Sept 22 🏔️ 
## The Epic Traverse: Puez-Odle Altopiano
**Base:** Val Gardena (Ortisei/Selva)


---

## ✅ Main Activity: The Best Day Hike in Val Gardena

**Expectation & Rationale:** 
Earth Trekkers considers this the single best hike in the Val Gardena region. It is a long, point-to-point journey across a massive, rocky alpine plateau with sweeping views in every direction. By taking the Dantercepies gondola up from Selva, you eliminate the massive initial climb, meaning most of your 17km day will be spent cruising along scenic ridges and gradually descending back into the valley. 

### 1. Puez-Odle Altopiano Hike
* **Distance**: 17 km (Point-to-point)
* **Elevation Change**: +400m ascent, -1,050m descent
* **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐ Moderate to Strenuous (Due to distance)
* **Duration**: 6–8 hours
* **Extensions**: Detour to the Col de Puez summit (+1 hour).
* **Resources**: 
  * [Earth Trekkers: Puez-Odle Altopiano](https://www.earthtrekkers.com/best-hikes-in-the-dolomites/)
  * [AllTrails: Puez-Odle Traverse](https://www.alltrails.com/explore?b_q=rifugio%20jimmi%20rifugio%20puez%20selva%20di%20val%20gardena)
* **Notes**: This is a point-to-point hike. You start at the top of the Dantercepies gondola in Selva, hike across the plateau to Rifugio Puez (perfect lunch stop), and hike all the way down Vallunga valley, ending right back in Selva.

## 6. Flex Day Options

# Day 11 — Wednesday, Sept 23 🏔️ 
## Flex Day 1 
**Base:** Val Gardena (Ortisei/Selva)

---

## ✅ Main Activity: Choose Your Own Adventure

**Expectation & Rationale:** 
Because we shifted your itinerary and ended the Cortina leg early, you have gained two extra "Flex Days" in Val Gardena. After completing a 17km hike yesterday, today is a perfect day to let your legs dictate the schedule. 

### Option 1: E-Bike the Sella Ronda
Rent e-mountain bikes in Selva and ride the famous Sella Ronda circuit. The e-bikes do the heavy lifting, allowing you to cover massive ground, cross four mountain passes, and enjoy continuous downhill runs.

### Option 2: The Sassolungo Loop (Hike)
If you are still eager for hiking, take the cable car up to Ciampinoi and hike the perimeter of the massive Sassolungo group. It's a relatively flat loop that wraps around the base of the towering peaks.
* **Resources**: [Earth Trekkers: Sassolungo Loop](https://www.earthtrekkers.com/best-hikes-in-the-dolomites/)

### Option 3: Via Ferrata Gran Cir (Introductory Climbing)
If you want to try a Via Ferrata without fully committing to a massive technical route, Gran Cir is a fantastic beginner route accessible from Passo Gardena. Rent a harness, via ferrata set, and helmet in town.
* **Resources**: [Earth Trekkers: Gran Cir](https://www.earthtrekkers.com/best-hikes-in-the-dolomites/)

### Option 4: Pure Relaxation
Spend the day in Ortisei. Visit the Mar Dolomit public pools, book a spa treatment at a local hotel, and shop along the pedestrian center.
